Vision & Mission Of the club

In the world full of rapidly advancing technology electronics waste, e-waste has become acritical concern. BIT E-Swachha, nestled within a vibrant community, is taking the lead inaddressing this issue.
A community committed to implementing effective and preventivemeasures of disposing and recycling e-waste to minimize environmental impact caused due to such wastes.
Sustainability and environmental consciousness being the focus our key objectives are:
● E-Waste management: On the mission of implementing proper disposal techniques of e-waste.
● E-collection drive: Simplifying responsible e-waste disposal.
● Awareness programs and tech events: To spread awareness of this cause and aspiring innovation through hackathons and competitions.

Club Inaugration

This potent vision was put into action starting off with a grand inauguration ceremony of the E-Swachha club by TEJASVI SURYA, honorable MP of South Bangalore on December 1st of 2024. His profound words and unwavering support furthered our cause. With great cause comes great responsibility hence the club president and leads were given their badges of their respective roles and from there the journey of bringing about a revolution in e-waste setfoot. The Inauguration ceremony created a impact by attracting a huge audience and also created a sense of civic responsibility among the masses on e-waste which motivated the students of BIT to join our club and embark on this journey of change together.

Industry Academia and Alumni Integration Summit 2024

Recycling e-waste is crucial in combating environmental damage by preventing the release of harmful materials into the environment. The BIT E-Swachha team's initiative in February 2024 to collect inoperative electronic gadgets from apartments and colleges was a significant step in creating awareness about the importance of reusing electronic devices. Electronic waste, if not recycled properly, can release hazardous substances like lead, mercury, and cadmium, which contaminate the environment and pose health risks to both humans and wildlife. The BIT E-Swachha team's efforts in organizing a collection drive demonstrate their commitment to addressing the e-waste issue and promoting responsible waste management practices. This initiative not only raises awareness but also encourages individuals to take proactive step.

E-waste management awareness walkathon at Ambedkar stadium

The e-Swachha BIT walkathon at Ambedkar Stadium in Bangalore was a resounding success. It aimed to raise awareness about e-waste disposal, emphasizing the importance of proper management like reducing, reusing, and recycling e-waste. Promoting e-swachhta is crucial, similar to the Swachh Bharat Abhiyan, to ensure a cleaner environment. With India ranking as the 3rd largest e-waste producer globally, taking responsibility for managing it is essential for a sustainable future. The walkathon, held on 12th May 2024, attracted a diverse audience, including teenagers, kids, senior citizens, and youth, effectively spreading the message of responsible e-waste disposal. Initiatives like this play a significant role in educating the public about the impact of e-waste on our health and the environment.

E-waste management awareness walkathon at Lalbagh

The Lalbagh Walkathon organized by the E-Swachha Club at BIT on May 25, 2024, was a successful event promoting e-waste awareness. The walkathon and skit performance engaged attendees, highlighting the importance of responsible e-waste management. Starting with a morning walkathon at 7 am and featuring Mr. Garudacharya's inspiring presence, the event encouraged community participation. The skit and awareness speech further educated the audience on e-waste practices, fostering positive change. The invitation to join the e-swachha community and the high level of participation reflect the event's success in spreading awareness and encouraging eco-friendly behaviors.

E-waste management awareness walkathon at Sankey tank

On May 26th, 2024, the E-Swachha club of BIT hosted a walkathon at Sankey Tank in Bangalore, featuring Dr. C. N. Manjunath as the chief guest. The event focused on educating people about electronic waste collection. The evening included engaging activities like a skit and dance performances that entertained and motivated the attendees. Dr. Manjunath's inspiring words resonated with the audience, emphasizing the importance of E-waste management. The walkathon around the scenic lake successfully raised awareness and showcased the club's dedication to environmental causes. Overall, the event was a great success, achieving its goal of spreading awareness and encouraging positive action towards E-waste management in the community.
